# Board Pitch

## Thesis (1 sentence)

AI Lifestyle Companion becomes the trusted behavior-change platform that helps people achieve measurable wellbeing outcomes through personalized coaching, longitudinal behavioral data, and continuous learning.

## The case

Most people know what healthy behaviors look like but struggle to sustain them over time. Existing health, fitness, and wellbeing apps excel at tracking activity and presenting information but fail to create accountability, personalization, and long-term behavior change. AI creates an opportunity to deliver individualized coaching at scale, making high-quality lifestyle guidance accessible to millions of people rather than only those who can afford human coaching.

## Why now

Generative AI has fundamentally changed consumer expectations from static applications to intelligent, always-available guidance. At the same time, users generate more lifestyle data than ever through wearables, health apps, and digital wellbeing tools. The opportunity is not to build another tracker, but to become the layer that converts fragmented health data into sustained behavior change. The next 24 months represent a unique opportunity to establish behavioral outcome data before platform providers such as Apple, Google, and OpenAI consolidate this category.

## What's defensible

The moat is not the AI conversation itself; that can be copied. The defensible asset is the behavioral outcomes data network. Through continuous coaching, user feedback, habit tracking, and outcome measurement, the platform learns which interventions work for which people under which circumstances. Over time, this creates proprietary behavior-change intelligence that improves coaching effectiveness, increases switching costs, and compounds into a durable competitive advantage.

## The economics

The business combines low-cost AI delivery with a subscription-based revenue model. AI serving costs are estimated at approximately €1.35 per active user per month using an 80/20 small-model-to-frontier-model routing strategy. The initial premium offering is priced at €9.99 per month, creating attractive gross margins that improve as retention increases. Long-term expansion opportunities include employer-sponsored wellbeing programs, coaching partnerships, and outcome-based offerings. The business succeeds through retention and engagement rather than customer acquisition alone.

## The risks

The largest risk is insufficient long-term engagement. Users may not perceive enough value beyond existing health and wellness solutions, resulting in weak retention and limited data accumulation. Platform providers such as Apple, Google, or OpenAI may launch competing experiences. If the product cannot demonstrate measurable behavior change, sustained engagement, and improved wellbeing outcomes, the investment case weakens significantly.

## Trust / failure modes

The system provides coaching, not diagnosis, treatment, or clinical decision-making. Unsafe health guidance represents the primary failure scenario. Mitigations include confidence signaling, transparent reasoning, evidence-based recommendations, safety guardrails, and human escalation paths for mental health concerns, self-harm indicators, eating disorder signals, or other high-risk situations. Reliability must consistently exceed established safety thresholds before broader scale.

## Scale / governance

As adoption grows, governance becomes increasingly important. The platform follows privacy-by-design principles, GDPR compliance, EU AI Act readiness, human oversight for high-risk situations, monthly safety reviews, quarterly model evaluations, and strict autonomy boundaries. Model portability through an abstraction layer reduces vendor dependency and supports long-term platform resilience.

## Competitive

The company competes with wellness applications, habit trackers, digital coaching platforms, and emerging AI health assistants. Differentiation comes from longitudinal behavioral profiling, personalized coaching, measurable outcome tracking, and a compounding behavioral data flywheel. The strategic objective is to own the behavior-change layer rather than the underlying AI models.

## The ask

Approve a 9-month validation program focused on proving product-market fit and retention.

Investment requested:

* Product development and AI infrastructure funding.
* One Product Lead.
* Two Full-Stack Engineers.
* One AI Engineer.
* Part-time Clinical/Safety Advisor.

Success criteria:

* ≥70% onboarding completion.
* ≥50% weekly coaching engagement.
* ≥25% 90-day retention.
* Demonstrated improvement in user wellbeing outcomes.

If these milestones are achieved, investment will continue toward building the Behavioral Outcomes Data Network, the primary long-term moat of the business.
